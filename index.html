Healthy Breakfast Pita Pizza: Under 250 Cals!

My apologies for posting two breakfast recipes in a row but this was SO yummy and easy I had to share with you guys! I make pita pizzas a lot and you'll probably be seeing more varieties.

Here's the breakfast pita pizza I made as a snack today:

  • 1 tbsp hummus
  • 1/2 cup kale
  • 1/4 cup egg whites
  • 1/2 tomato
  • 1 MorningStar breakfast sausage
  • 1 Joseph's Low Carb Pita Bread

Cook the egg whites (in microwave or stovetop). Heat up the breakfast sausage. Steam the kale a bit to soften it. Toast the pita bread*. Spread hummus on toasted pita, top with kale, tomatoes, egg whites, and crumbled breakfast sausage!

* Can be done in a toaster oven, oven, or if you toast two sides in a regular toaster

Macros: (210cals) 6 F, 13 C, 26 P

Ginger Nama Shoyu Adventurer Bowl

Ok so I originally actually made this a while ago and it was published through a different platform but I decided to share it with you guys here because I know how much everyone in the area LOVES Life Alive. Their meals are hearty,balanced, filling, and so nutritious! P.S. Not sure why I thought editing my pictures like this was a good idea so I apologize for that…

It seems like everyone who goes their has a “go-to” and the Adventurer is mine! So one day I decided to try to remake it. Here‘s the image they have on their website. Here is my version:

I love to order “The Adventurer” in a wrap so that I can get everything in one bite. I highly suggest you make it like this. I didn't have any wraps in the kitchen so instead I served it on a plate with small tortillas. The colors alone should make you want to try this. It is one of my absolute favorite meals. SOO GOOD!

Life Alive's Sesame Ginger Nama Shoyu Dressing (1-2 servings) adapted from SarahFit:

  • 1 garlic glove
  • 1″ freshly minced/chopped ginger
  • 1/2 tbsp tamari (or soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp water

The Adventurer (1 serving) adapted from Life Alive:

  • 2 leaves Kale
  • 1/2 cup corn
  • 1 beet
  • 1/2 cup broccoli, steamed
  • 1/2 cup baby/shredded/chopped carrots, steamed
  • 1 oz cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa & brown rice blend*
  • 1 tbsp almonds

 

Cook the beet by boiling it in water for about 10-15 mins. Chop almonds and roast them slightly to bring out flavor. Chop kale and steam broccoli & baby carrots. Add cheese to cooked quinoa and brown rice blend. Combine all ingredients in a wrap, or serve on a plate with tortilla slices on the side.

*I used Seeds of Change ready to heat packs, but you can make your own. I suggest 3/4 cup brown rice to 1/4 cup quinoa.

Healthy Fast Food Cheeseburger

Under 300cals, high in fiber, and dairy free!

This was literally SO good and took me maybe 15 minutes to make (hence the “fast food”). Better than your average burger, low in fat, and packed with nutrients! As many of you know I'm lactose intolerant so I made a dairy free version using these go veggie! lactose free cheese slices, but feel free to use whatever cheese you'd like (the macros are pretty similar to real cheese).

I really don't know what else to say about it because you just have to go make it so let's get to the recipe…

  • 40g mushrooms
  • 40g onions
  • 4 oz extra lean ground turkey
  • 1 slice cheese
  • 125g sweet potato

Shape your ground turkey into a patty and place in a pan with some PAM. In a separate pan, sauté your mushroom and onions while your burger cooks. Slice the sweet potato in rounds, pierce some holes in he rounds, and wrap in a damp paper towel. Microwave for 2 mins. Once the burger is ready, turn off the heat, add cheese, cover and let sit so the cheese can melt. Stack half of the rounds on the plate, add the burger, mushrooms and onions, and top with the second half of the sweet potato.

I topped mine with tomato slices and BBQ sauce, and served steamed broccoli on the side.

Macros for the burger stack: 293 cals (5g fat, 30g carbs (5g fiber), 34g protein)

Paleo Fruit Cake


For those of you who follow me on Instagram, you may have seen the paleo bread I made this weekend! The recipe is super simple so I thought I would share it with you all.

I literally substituted anything I could find in my kitchen for the actual ingredients, which shows how versatile the recipe is! Every over-ripened fruit was used up that I could find! For example, I used mango puree for applesauce, plums for peaches, agave nectar for sugar, and almond flour for coconut flour. The result? Nutty, moist, and refreshing deliciousness!

Here is what I used, but feel free to tweak it to what you have available in the kitchen. Let me know your fruit cake substitutions in the comments!

  • 1 peach
  • 1 plum
  • 1½ teaspoons vanilla extract, divided
  • 1 1/2 cup almond flour
  • ¾ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • ⅓ cup mango puree (*or unsweetened applesauce)
  • ¼ cup agave nectar
  • 3 tablespoons melted coconut oil
  • dash of coconut sugar

Preheat oven 350F. Dice the plum and 1/2 the peach, toss in 1/2 tsp vanilla extract and a little coconut sugar. Place in oven and bake for ~10 mins until caramelized. Meanwhile, combine all dry ingredients in a bowl (the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt). In a separate bowl mix the rest of the vanilla extract (1 tsp), eggs, puree, agave, and coconut oil. Stir the dry ingredients into the wet, and fold in the caramelized fruit from the oven. Bake at 350F for 45-50 mins!

 

Quick and Healthy Turkey Chili

Ok I was SO proud of myself for making this chili recipe. I took a bunch of leftovers (yet again) and recreated it into a delicious dinner in less than 15 minutes. My leftovers included: opened bags of frozen carrots & corn, and an opened can of black beans. I added a can of tomatoes and voila!
I couldn't believe how quick and easy this was! Literally throw this in a pot when you get home from work, go change into some comfy clothes and it'll be done when you're back. I'm going to post below the recipe for one serving, but feel free to double up on the ingredients etc.

  • 4 oz. 99% Extra Lean Ground Turkey
  • 1/2 cup Low Sodium Black Beans
  • 1/4 Onions (Diced)
  • 1/2 cup No Salt Added Diced Tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup Corn
  • 1/4 cup Carrots
  • 1/2 tsp Chili Powder
  • 1 Clove Garlic, Chopped
  • Dash of Cumin, Cayenne pepper, Stevia
  • Salt (to taste)

Topping ideas:

  • Romaine Lettuce
  • Greek Yogurt
  • Cheese
  • Hot Sauce

Spray a sauce pan with PAM and saute onions, garlic, and turkey. Once they have browned a little, add the spices and rest of ingredients. Add a dash of water and bring to boil. Reduce heat to a simmer and let cook for 10 mins. Remember to taste the spices after 5 mins and adjust them accordingly!

Macros: 300 cals (2g F, 31g C, 37g P) w/ 10g of fiber !

Sweet Grain Chicken Bowl


For anyone who read my post about why you should eat white rice, I mentioned that one of my FAVORITE ways to get in a nutrient dense meal while spiking insulin levels was this sweet grain bowl.

It's so easy to make, smells delicious, and tastes even better. Trust me, the combination sounds weird but is so, so, good.

post agave syrup…pre cinnamon

This can easily be served without the chicken to be made vegan, doubled/tripled, or you can serve it atop greens on a salad!

  • 1/2 cup diced sweet potato (100g)
  • 4 oz chicken breast
  • 1 tbsp raisins (14g)
  • 1/2 cup brown rice, cooked (100g)
  • 1 tsp agave nectar (4g)

While brown rice is cooking, dice sweet potato. Wrap in damp paper towel and microwave ~2.5 mins. Spray a pan with PAM and cook the chicken breast. Combine all ingredients into a  bowl, sprinkle with cinnamon, and drizzle agave.

*I like to also add half a packet of stevia to make it extra sweet.

Macros: 290 calories 1.5F/39C/30P
